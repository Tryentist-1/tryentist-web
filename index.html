Let's Try It!

Welcome! If you know me, you know I love diving into things – whether it's the rules of D&D, the physics of archery, the vibes of a live show, or the collaborative spirit of Burning Man. My latest deep dive? The fascinating, sometimes strange, always evolving universe of AI.

This site is currently my basecamp for that expedition. As a long-time tech enthusiast and sci-fi/fantasy fan, I'm exploring how these new AI tools actually work, what they can create, and where they might take us. It's part world-building, part coding, part pure experimentation.

Forget polished corporate techno babble. Expect hands-on projects, half-baked ideas, code that mostly works (check out the Archery Scorer below!), and honest thoughts as I try things out. Think of it like setting up camp on the digital playa – focused on the build, the process, and seeing what cool stuff emerges from the dust.

So, pull up a virtual laboratory stool and watch the process unfold. It's bound to be an interesting trip. If you are a Tryentist too, ping me and let me know so we can compare notes!

Featured Experiment: AI-Assisted Creation

Example: The interactive Olympic Archery Scorer, built using p5.js with significant code generation assistance from Google Gemini. I created the first version of the app with a single prompt and in less than an hour of revisions with just comments back and forth I created the version below to use with my archers. The visual interface lets you see how your arrows are grouping, and what your score could be if you can get your group to be centered on the bullseye. I have wanted something like this for literally 20 years and today I made it in less than 30 minutes.
